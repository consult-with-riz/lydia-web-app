Anchoring Bias
Strong
Client is anchored on the original purchase price, making it hard to evaluate current value objectively. The reference point is emotional, not rational.
"If you were seeing this home for the first time today, what would feel like a fair price given where you are now?"
Present Bias
Strong
Client repeatedly deflects from long-term planning to immediate security needs, discounting future outcomes in favour of short-term certainty.
Anchor on a near-term milestone (6–12 months) rather than 10-year outcomes.
Loss Aversion
Moderate
The pain of potential loss is outweighing the potential benefit of action. She is in a defensive, protection-oriented state.
Reframe around what she is protecting rather than what she might gain.

Client archetype — Sarah Johnson
🌫️
The Avoider
Money decisions feel heavy or anxiety-inducing. Sarah prefers not to engage — which means important decisions get delayed, and discomfort builds quietly over time.
Communication style that works: slow down, name the discomfort directly, never rush toward a decision. She needs to feel safe before she can engage.
What she hears when you say "we need to decide": threat. Reframe as exploration — "What feels right to look at first?" is far more accessible.
Her core fear: making an irreversible mistake. Acknowledge that mistakes are survivable and reversibility can be built into decisions together.
What builds trust fastest: not pushing. Every time you sit with her avoidance rather than trying to fix it, her trust in you deepens measurably.
