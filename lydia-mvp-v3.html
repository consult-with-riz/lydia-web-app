🧠 Bias Detector
Paste in your notes from a client meeting. Lydia reads them and surfaces the behavioural patterns it detects.
Paste your meeting notes
3 behavioural patterns detected
Anchoring Bias
Strong signal
Client is cognitively anchored on the original purchase price of the family home, making it hard to evaluate its current value objectively. The reference point is emotional, not rational.
Try asking: "If you were seeing this home for the first time today, what would feel like a fair price given everything you've described about your life now?"
Present Bias
Strong signal
Client repeatedly deflects from long-term planning to immediate security needs. She is discounting future outcomes in favour of short-term certainty — a classic present-bias pattern.
Anchor the conversation on a near-term milestone (6–12 months) rather than 10-year outcomes to reduce psychological distance.
Loss Aversion
Moderate signal
The repeated concern about "decisions she might regret" suggests the pain of potential loss is outweighing the potential benefit of action. She is in a defensive, protection-oriented state.
Reframe choices around what she is protecting rather than what she might gain. "What matters most to keep safe?" is more accessible than "What do you want to build?"

Client archetype — Sarah Johnson
🌫️
The Avoider
Money decisions feel heavy or anxiety-inducing. Sarah prefers not to engage — which means important decisions get delayed, and discomfort builds quietly over time.
Communication style that works: slow down, name the discomfort directly, never rush toward a decision. She needs to feel safe before she can engage.
What she hears when you say "we need to decide": threat. Reframe decisions as explorations. "What feels right to look at first?" is more accessible than "we need to make a call on this."
Her core fear: making an irreversible mistake. Acknowledge that mistakes are survivable and reversibility is a real thing you can build into decisions together.
The thing that builds trust fastest: not pushing. Every time you sit with her avoidance rather than trying to fix it, her trust in you deepens by a measurable degree.
